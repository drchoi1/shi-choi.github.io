How to get here

Mia Nha Trang is located in central Vietnam, accessible via Cam Ranh International Airport (CXR).

  • It is about a 20-minute car ride from CXR to Mia Nha Trang. We recommend downloading the Grab rideshare app on your phone to easily call a car, approximately $10 one-way.
  • Alternatively, you can also arrange car pick-up service directly with Mia. Additional hotel charge applies.

Please Read! General travel guide when booking flights

  • Ideally, your first port of entry into Vietnam should be CXR.
  • We discourage an international arrival into SGN, HAN, or DAD with a 2-3 hour layover before continuing onward on a domestic leg to CXR.
  • If your first port of entry is one of Vietnam's major international airports, such as SGN, HAN, or DAD, we highly recommend spending a day or two there before continuing on a domestic flight to CXR, due to:
    • Long immigration lines are expected at major international airports such as SGN, HAN, and DAD.
    • Even with a 3-hour layover, you may miss your connecting flight from international arrival to domestic transfer departure to CXR.
    • Even with a through ticket under one airline alliance, luggage will not be checked through to CXR. You will need to pick up luggage after immigration, change terminals, and re-check with the domestic leg, such as Vietnam Airlines.

Traveling from United States

We recommend the following itinerary:

  • Outbound itinerary to Nha Trang (CXR): Take Korean Air: SFO/LAX/JFK/BOS - ICN - CXR.
    • 2-3 hour layover at ICN with luggage checked through to final destination CXR
    • If time allows, we recommend a few-day stopover in Seoul to enjoy the prime cherry blossom season before continuing on from ICN nonstop to CXR.
    • You can also travel down south to Busan for amazing Korean seafood and take a short nonstop flight from PUS to CXR.
  • Return itinerary to United States: After the wedding, take advantage of 10-day visa-free transit for U.S. citizens (see details). Make sure your total transit visit time in China is less than 240 hours (10 days).
    • Take a short nonstop flight from CXR to Chengdu (TFU) for about 4 days in the city for pandas, spicy Sichuan hotpot, Gaydu, tea cultures, and more; then continue on to either Beijing or Shanghai for about 5 days. Both cities have several flight options to return to the U.S.
    • Or take a short nonstop flight from CXR to Guangzhou (CAN) for super high-tech experiences in the cyberpunk cities of the Greater Bay Area of Guangzhou and Shenzhen. After Shenzhen, you can exit China on a 30-minute bullet train to visit Hong Kong for a few days before flying from HKG to the U.S. with plenty of flight options.
  • Please note: Hong Kong (HKG), Japan (NRT or HND), and Taiwan (TPE) do not operate nonstop service to CXR, which typically involves an international arrival transfer to domestic at either SGN, HAN, or DAD. We caution against this itinerary. See reasons in the travel guide above.
