Our story

2023

Chapter 1 The usual beginning...

July: First message

Hee Joong ghosts Calvin, thinking 'Must be a Chinese scammer... (or maybe not?)'

October: The restart after three full months

Calvin tried again, and our journey started.

2024

Chapter 2 A leap of faith

January: Flying into the unknown

Only to find the one in a skip trip to Lake Tahoe.

Calvin's secret wish

Facing the beautiful, serene Lake Tahoe sunrise, Calvin secretly wished this moment could last a little longer, knowing Hee Joong had to return to school…

Chapter 3 The Mexican sunset

June: First overseas trip to Puerto Vallarta, Mexico, with friends

Along with An and Matt, they take on a Puerto Vallarta trip to kick off the Pride Month!

Hee Joong found a local boat tour deal and put everyone's name on board for a full day trip.

On their return boat ride, Calvin mesmerizes at the sunset and officially asks Hee Joong to be his boyfriend.

And Hee Joong said yes! That was June 4th, 2024.

Chapter 4 Calvin's first visit to Seoul

July: he instantly falls in love with 'the big city' alongwith Hee Joong

Calvin and Hee Joong experience locals' support for their love. Ajummas and halmeonis in small alleyways always praised their relationship and Hee Joong had to pretend not to have understood them (he was blushing).

Calvin in one of the major gay districts in Seoul: Jongno.

Besides Calvin's discovery of 首尔 as a city of culture and tradition, he also had a chance to meet with Hee Joong's mom for the first time (over a dinner in Myeongdong).

Chapter 5 Six Senses Ninh Van Bay

December: X-mas gifting for disabled children in Nha Trang

Calvin's constant longing for a vacation in Southeast Asia was not always aligning with Hee Joong's academic calendar. That meant Hee Joong sometimes had to work at the most beautiful sunset. Writing was painful but the view was deeply healing, he recalls.

The GM at Six Senses, Greg, asked if they wanted to join the Christmas present gifting event at a local orphanage. So moved by the children's pure eyes and joyful silences (many of them were deaf), their special attachment for Nha Trang grew…

2025

Chapter 6 Hee Joong's first visit to Chengdu

January: Hee Joong experienced deep hospitality of Chengdu and love of Calvin’s big family

Hee Joong had little idea how he'd be received by Calvin's family. With little knowledge of Chinese language and Chengdu people, he was deeply moved by everyone in Calvin's extended family.

Even Calvin's nieces liked Hee Joong a lot. Hee Joong was already feeling like he became part of the Wang family (Calvin's maternal side)!

Hee Joong's first visit was filled with few words and lots of loving emotions. He felt like he had to reciprocate and, as a linguist, he delivered his first speech in Mandarin Chinese in front of Calvin's extended family on the new year's day.

Chapter 7 Advancing academic milestones amidst travels

July: Advancing academic milestones amidst travels

Even when Hee Joong was traveling across the globe with Calvin, he read and wrote whenever possible.

In July 2025, Hee Joong passed a major milestone, his first linguistics generals paper, while in Chengdu (he had written and submitted his draft while in Koh Samui, Thailand).

When the defense was over, it was 10pm local time. Hee Joong and Calvin went straight out to the gay club "Butterfly" in Chengdu to celebrate, with his cousins Liu Rui and Wang Liu.

Chapter 8 Welcoming families to the U.S.
August: Wang Liu visits

Wang Liu, one of Calvin's sibling-like cousins, visited SF. They went on a Hwy 1 roadtrip up to Mendocino, CA.

Hee Joong organized Calvin's b-day celebration by marking Calvin's age in arm-marked Chinese characters.

Much luck for Wang Liu for having a sunny day in SF, known for the coldest summer!

September: Hee Joong's mom visits

Hee Joong's mom visits West Coast, making stop in SF. Calvin being the best guide possible (visible in Hee Joong's sunglasses)

A classic tour of the Golden Gate Bridge on a sunny (& windy) day never gets old!

The night before Hee Joong and the mom's departure, the three opened up and deeply connected as a family. What a progress since the year before!

December: Calvin's parents visit and a joint trip to Hawai'i

Calvin's parents visit the U.S. finally after the Covid. They both look like re-living their youth.

Hee Joong found their favorite sunset spot on the Hawai'i Island. Just one of the innumerous beautiful sunset pictures, shining with pure joy.

This off-road adventure to Waikolu Valley (on the Moloka'i Island) would not have been possible without the preceding 13 days of bonding in the beautiful Hawai'i.

2026

Chapter 9 Two years later... back to Lake Tahoe

January: the Wedding proposal

Calvin's secret wish from two years ago (see Ch. 1) became a plan in Fall 2025. He had planned his proposal to become a full cycle of their relationship in Lake Tahoe, timing the sunset angle, inviting his dear friend Alex as photographer, and asking his parents for their blessings.

After Hee Joong said YES, they enjoyed the very special sunset. All Hee Joong could do is cry out of sheer happiness and say Meixiangdao (没想到; 'I never expected this to come true') to Calvin's parents.

Fine dining dinner followed, perpetuating their special memories of Lake Tahoe.

Chapter 10 Blessed in Blossoms

March: Family trip to Mokpo and received Hee Joong parents' blessings

Following the wedding proposal, there was the final milestone before the ceremony: receiving blessings from Hee Joong's parents. For this purpose, they went on a weekend trip to the Southwest of Korea: Mokpo.

At Youngsanjae, Hee Joong's family and Calvin had a deep bonding dinner. Calvin promised to take a good care of Hee Joong while also being treated with a physical therapy by the dad.

Best lesson learned: care is the key to the family of love.

It was late March, when cherry blossoms started to bloom from the South. Finally, Calvin and Hee Joong were blessed in blossoms.

How to get here

Mia Nha Trang is located in central Vietnam, accessible via Cam Ranh International Airport (CXR).

  • It is about a 30-minute car ride from CXR to Mia Nha Trang. We recommend downloading the Grab rideshare app on your phone to easily call a car, approximately $10 one-way.
  • Alternatively, you can also arrange car pick-up service directly with Mia. Additional hotel charge applies.

Please Read! General travel guide when booking flights

  • Ideally, your first port of entry into Vietnam should be CXR.
  • We discourage an international arrival into SGN, HAN, or DAD with a 2-3 hour layover before continuing onward on a domestic leg to CXR.
  • If your first port of entry is one of Vietnam's major international airports, such as SGN, HAN, or DAD, we highly recommend spending a day or two there before continuing on a domestic flight to CXR.
    • Long immigration lines are expected at major international airports such as SGN, HAN, and DAD.
    • Even with a 3-hour layover, you may miss your connecting flight from international arrival to domestic transfer departure to CXR.
    • Even with a through ticket under one airline alliance, luggage will not be checked through to CXR. You will need to pick up luggage after immigration, change terminals, and re-check with the domestic leg, such as Vietnam Airlines.

From East/West Coast of United States

We highly recommend Korean Air: SFO/LAX/JFK/BOS - ICN - CXR, considering:

  • 2-3 hour layover at ICN
  • luggage checked through to final destination CXR
  • If time allows, we highly recommend a few-day stopover in Seoul to enjoy cherry blossoms before continuing on from ICN nonstop to CXR.
  • Please note: Hong Kong (HKG), Japan (NRT or HND), and Taiwan (TPE) do not operate nonstop service to CXR, which typically involves an international arrival transfer to domestic at either SGN, HAN, or DAD. We caution against this itinerary. See reasons in the travel guide above.
